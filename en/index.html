The Story So Far

What the first film left behind — and the question it raised

Hope Harbor, a fishing village near the DMZ. On a night when troops are pulled away to fight a wildfire and communications go dark, something unknown arrives. Outpost chief Beom-seok and rookie officer Sung-ae try to protect a village of elders, while Sung-ki and the townsfolk who went into the hills to hunt the creature become the hunted instead. A disaster born of not-knowing spills through human conflict into cosmic tragedy.

Ending spoilers ahead.

The ending — not an invasion, but a search

They were never invaders. Empress J'aur came searching for her lost young son — and the child was already dead, killed by a human, Yang-bae. At the climax, as Sung-ki topples the warrior Ma'veyyo atop a speeding police car, the car slams into a road sign and Sung-ki seems to fall to his death. In the same moment the alien mothership detonates in the sky and sinks into the sea off Hope Harbor.

In despair, Ma'veyyo tears out his own heart and offers it to the Empress, and J'aur speaks a line borrowed from Hebrews 11:1 — faith is the evidence of things hoped for. Then the dead child returns to life, and the meaning of the title HOPE is complete.

One post-credits scene — Sung-ki, thought dead, limps away, broken but alive. The one living witness. The first film closes here, but beneath the waves the sunken ship, the revived child, and the offered heart all remain.

※ The exact wording of the film's key line is reported slightly differently across sources (a borrowing from Hebrews 11:1). Until re-confirmed, this site marks it as "based on audience accounts."

A Fan's Sequel · Unofficial fan scenario

HOPE 2 : The Evidence

A fan scenario woven straight from the last frame of the first film — the sunken ship, the offered heart, the revived child, the surviving Sung-ki. A non-profit fan creation, not official canon.

Act 1

The Sunken Star

The sea off Hope Harbor, where the mothership went down. Military cordons and the eyes of the world converge. Sung-ki, barely alive, testifies — the Empress, the heart, the revived child — and no one believes him. The "faith" theme of the first film inverts: now the one who saw is asked to prove it. Then, from the ship below, a faint heartbeat signal is detected.

Act 2

Second Contact

The military launches salvage and tech-recovery operations, but the ship is not dead. Ma'veyyo's heart resonates with the ship's core, cradling the child Kali back to life. A fleet from the Gh'ertu homeworld arrives to investigate — their only record reads "humans killed the heir." All-out war between aliens and humans begins, and against a government bent on covering it up, Sung-ki, Sung-ae and Beom-seok reunite.

Act 3

The Evidence

At the height of the war, the revived Kali rises from the sea. The child who holds Ma'veyyo's heart — a living proof of sacrifice and forgiveness. Empress J'aur chooses, instead of revenge, to believe what the child shows her. "Then, what was hoped for could not be seen. Now, this child is the evidence of what we hoped for." It closes leaving room for coexistence between human and alien. Post-credits — the empty Gh'ertu throne. A new threat foretold.

Loose ends, tied

The offered heart → the heart is a vessel of life and faith; it becomes the engine of resurrection.

How the child returns → resonance between the ship's core and the heart.

Sung-ki's survival scene → the axis of the sequel. "The witness who saw."

The "evidence of things hoped for" line → the thesis and subtitle of the whole sequel.
